Organizations rarely struggle because they lack enthusiasm for AI. They struggle because adoption outruns governance, operating models, and clear business framing.

The practical path is to treat AI work as a product capability with architecture guardrails:

1. define measurable value hypotheses;
2. limit the operational blast radius;
3. instrument for quality and drift;
4. clarify data handling and security constraints;
5. put human accountability back into critical decisions.

The result is not slower innovation. It is innovation with enough structure to scale responsibly.
