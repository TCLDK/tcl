Security becomes friction when it arrives late, inconsistently, or without context. It becomes an accelerator when it is built into engineering workflows, platforms, and architectural defaults.

A few patterns consistently help:

- paved-road infrastructure for secure defaults;
- automated verification in build and deployment pipelines;
- threat-informed architecture reviews focused on real risk;
- clear ownership between platform, product, and security teams.

This is as much an organizational design problem as it is a tooling problem.
